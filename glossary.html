Comprehensive Trading Glossary (A → Z)

Quick definitions for traders. Copy these into tooltips or learning cards.

ASK
The price sellers are asking for the asset or contract.
BID
The price buyers are willing to pay.
BLOCK
Large institutional order executed off-exchange or specially handled.
CALL
Option giving the right to buy the underlying at a strike.
PUT
Option giving the right to sell the underlying at a strike.
DELTA
Sensitivity of option price to a $1 move in the underlying (directional exposure).
GAMMA
Rate of change of delta as the underlying moves.
THETA
Time decay — how much value an option loses each day.
VEGA
Sensitivity of option price to implied volatility changes.
IMPLIED VOLATILITY (IV)
Market's expected future volatility priced into options.
IV RANK
Measure of where current IV sits relative to its historical range.
OPEN INTEREST
Number of outstanding contracts for a strike/expiration.
LIQUIDITY
Availability of buyers/sellers; high liquidity means tighter spreads and better fills.
SLIPPAGE
Difference between expected and executed price due to market moves or low liquidity.
STOP-LOSS
Order type or rule to exit a losing trade to prevent further loss.
TA (Technical Analysis)
Trading based on price patterns, indicators, and chart structure.
FA (Fundamental Analysis)
Trading based on company financials, macroeconomic indicators, and news.
RISK/REWARD
Ratio of potential loss to potential gain (example 1:2).
ROLL
Move an option position to a different strike or expiration (out or up/down).
LOTTO
Cheap, short-dated option with low probability but high payoff potential.
SWEEP
Aggressive multi-exchange option buy that sweeps the book — often signals institutional flow.
VIX
Volatility index — market’s expectation of S&P 500 volatility over 30 days.
WICK
The tail of a candle showing rejection at a price.
VWAP
Volume Weighted Average Price — intraday benchmark for buyers/sellers.
SHARPE RATIO
Risk-adjusted return measure; higher is generally better.
REMIT
(Context) Fill and execution shorthand used in chat / groups.
